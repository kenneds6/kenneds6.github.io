My Story

I am currently a graduate student at the University of Cincinnati pursuing a Master of Science degree in Computer Science.

My current research interests include network security and applied cryptography. My research advisor is Boyang Wang, Ph.D.

How Did I Get Here?

In 2013, I graduated from the University of Cincinnati, summa cum laude, with a bachelor's degree in history. When I began college in 2009, my family encouraged me to get a broad liberal arts education and I was drawn to history as a major because of my interest in the subject. I was afforded the opportunity to go beyond the routine curriculum by researching, writing and presenting a paper as a Charles Phelps Taft Fellow in history. You can find out more about that work in the My Work section of this website. While I enjoyed history and the liberal arts education, I never intended to pursue history beyond college. Instead, I was focusing on applying to graduate programs in atmospheric science. While visiting one of the schools that I expected to apply to, a professor advised me to take a computer science class. During my senior year, I took a yearlong course in Java programming and the fundamentals of computer science, which piqued my interest in the field. Unfortunately, I was not able to take more computer science classes because I was graduating from the university. I began to study programming languages (such as C and C++) and CS concepts on my own. While I believe that it is possible to learn on your own, I value the educational experience that comes from having interactions between fellow students and with professors. That led me to pursue computer science study in a more formal setting at the University of Cincinnati.
